Abstract

We introduce a new item for bucket-listing:See the Urban Dictionary for a definition:
To go out and experience the things on your bucket list. To be adventurous and spontaneous. eating dishes that represent 200 cuisines. This is distinct, and we believe more rewarding, than focussing on say the list of countries or top Michelin restaurants. The rules, if you want to call them so, are simple. First, a cuisine must be catalogued (known as the spreadsheet), second be supplied by a reasonably authentic purveyor (and we offer more guidance on how), and third, documented in some digital form. We show how this can be done in New York City with a sample of local restaurants.

There are several limitations, notably, the definition of what is a cuisine. To clarify, we selected a non-exhaustive, non-systematic list that serves the purpose of a cuisine cyclopedia. Introduction

There are an estimated 20,000-24,0000 restaurants and eateries in New York City. The variety around the world is even more overwhelming. Some diets are eve recognized as part of the UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage Lists such as the Mediterranean diet.

A word on what the 200 cuisines is not:

  • It is not an eating competition. Such endeavours are in the domain of the Coney Island Hot Dog Eating Contest and, we think, disgusting.
  • It is not a food marathon. Speed does not matter. For a food marathon defined as 42 dishes in 24 hours look no further than our Jackson Heights restaurant crawl.

We also introduce our personal rating scheme of how we liked it. It serves best as a predictor if we would come back. Gone are the ubiquitous five stars (☆☆☆☆☆) and welcomed is the emoji scale.
